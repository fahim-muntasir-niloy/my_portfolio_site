Majority Voting Ensemble Approach for Predicting Diabetes Mellitus in Female Patients from Unbalanced Dataset

Fahim Muntasir, Md. Shamim Anower, Md. Nahiduzzaman

Diabetes is a common yet deadly disease among women. In 2017, about one in every nine women were found to be exposed to diabetes in the USA alone. It is needless to say the importance to predict it before it can turn deadlier for the body. In this paper, we used machine learning algorithms to predict the possibility of diabetes in women based on the behavioral and medical diagnosis data related to diabetes.

Improving Performance Factors of an Imbalanced Credit Risk Dataset using SMOTE

Manisha Karim, M. F. Samad, Fahim Muntasir

In this paper, a supervised machine learning approach for predicting the credit risk of a loan applicant has been presented. Due to the imbalance of real-world datasets, high accuracy but low precision, recall, and F1-scores may be obtained. This paper aims to solve this issues by using an oversampling technique called Synthetic Minority Over-sampling Technique (SMOTE).
